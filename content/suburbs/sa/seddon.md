facts:
- Seddon is a locality in the Australian state of South Australia located on Yorke
  Peninsula about 130 kilometres west of the state capital of Adelaide.
- Seddon is located within the federal Division of Grey, the state electoral district
  of Narungga and the local government area of the Yorke Peninsula Council.
- The boundaries for the locality were created in 1999 for the long established name.
- Seddon is located within the cadastral Hundred of Cunningham.
- Land use within Seddon is divided between primary production and conservation, the
  latter being concerned with the coastline overlooking Investigator Strait.
- The historic former Seddon School building, now a private residence, is listed on
  the South Australian Heritage Register.
- The locality includes the protected area known as the Leven Beach Conservation Park.
- The coastline from Seddon to Corny Point is popular with rock and beach fishers.
- The beaches are generally sandy interspersed with reefs, and with some rocky headlands.
- Dolphins have been sighted from the shore at Seddon.
