facts:
- Wudinna is located on the Eyre Peninsula in South Australia.
- The town is named after a granite outcrop, Wudinna Hill, which is a prominent landmark
  in the area.
- Wudinna is known as the 'Granite Gateway' to the Gawler Ranges.
- The area around Wudinna is rich in Aboriginal history and culture.
- The Wudinna district is primarily agricultural, with wheat and sheep farming being
  prominent.
- The town hosts the yearly Wudinna Agricultural Show.
- Wudinna is home to Australia's largest collection of outdoor sculptures, known as
  the Australian Farmer Sculptures.
- The granite outcrops around Wudinna are popular for rock climbing and bushwalking.
- Wudinna experiences a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and cool, wet
  winters.
- The local economy is supported by agriculture, tourism, and mining.
