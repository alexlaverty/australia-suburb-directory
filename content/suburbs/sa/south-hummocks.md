- South Hummocks is located in the Yorke Peninsula Council area.
- It is situated approximately 150km west of Adelaide, the capital city of South Australia.
- The area is primarily known for its agricultural activities, particularly grain
  farming.
- The Hummocks range, a series of low hills, traverses the region giving the town
  its name.
- South Hummocks is close to the coastline, offering access to beaches and fishing.
- The town has a small population, contributing to a rural and quiet atmosphere.
- Nearby towns include Kadina, Wallaroo, and Moonta, which offer additional services
  and amenities.
- The region experiences a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and cool, wet
  winters.
- The area has a history of copper mining dating back to the 19th century.
- South Hummocks is part of the Narungga Aboriginal nation traditional lands.
