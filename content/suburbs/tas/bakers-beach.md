- Bakers Beach is a coastal suburb of Tasmania, Australia, located on the north-west
  coast.
- It is approximately a 20-minute drive (21km) north-west of Ulverstone.
- Bakers Beach is known for its wide, sandy beach, which is popular for swimming,
  surfing, fishing, and walking.
- The area around Bakers Beach is known for its rich Aboriginal heritage, with evidence
  of past occupation and middens still visible in some areas.
- The suburb is named after George Baker, one of the first settlers in the area in
  the mid-1800s.
- Bakers Beach is a popular holiday destination, with many holiday homes and caravan
  parks in the area.
- The Rubicon Estuary, which is a significant wetland area, is located nearby.
- The suburb experiences a mild, temperate climate.
- Bakers Beach offers views of the Bass Strait and Table Cape.
- The local economy is largely based on tourism and fishing.
