facts:
- Barrow Island is located off the northwest coast of Western Australia.
- It is entirely dedicated to nature conservation and industrial activities related
  to oil and gas production.
- The island is a Class A Nature Reserve.
- It's known for its flat terrain and arid climate.
- Barrow Island is an important nesting site for flatback turtles.
- The island is home to several endemic species, including the Barrow Island euro
  (a type of wallaby).
- It's one of Australia's most important oil and gas production sites.
- Chevron operates the Gorgon gas project on Barrow Island.
- Strict quarantine measures are in place to protect the island's fragile ecosystem.
- Human access to the island is highly restricted.
