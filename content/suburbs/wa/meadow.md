- Meadow Springs is a suburb of Mandurah, located approximately 75km south of Perth,
  Western Australia.
- The area was originally known as 'The Meadow', and the name 'Meadow Springs' was
  adopted in the late 1980s.
- A significant portion of Meadow Springs is situated on reclaimed swampland.
- Meadow Springs is bordered by the Peel Inlet to the east and the Indian Ocean to
  the west.
- The suburb features a golf course designed by Graham Marsh.
- Meadow Springs is home to several parks and reserves, including Meadow Springs Reserve
  and Samphire Cove Reserve.
- The suburb has a mix of housing options, including single-family homes, townhouses,
  and apartments.
- Meadow Springs is served by the Mandurah railway line, with a station located within
  the suburb.
- The population of Meadow Springs has grown significantly in recent decades.
- Meadow Springs is part of the City of Mandurah local government area.
