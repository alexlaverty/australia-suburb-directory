facts:
- Derby is located in the Kimberley region of Western Australia.
- The town experiences a tropical savanna climate with distinct wet and dry seasons.
- Derby holds the record for the highest tides in Australia, reaching up to 11.8 meters.
- The Boab Prison Tree, a large hollow boab tree once used as a temporary lockup for
  Indigenous prisoners, is a significant landmark near Derby.
- Derby is a gateway to the Gibb River Road, a popular 4WD route.
- The Mowanjum Festival, celebrating Indigenous art and culture, is held annually
  near Derby.
- Derby was once a major port for the West Kimberley, but its importance declined
  after the development of Wyndham.
- The town is situated on the edge of the King Sound, known for its diverse marine
  life.
- Horizontal Falls, a natural phenomenon created by tidal currents squeezing through
  narrow gorges, is a popular tourist attraction accessible from Derby.
- Derby is home to a diverse range of bird species, making it a popular destination
  for birdwatchers.
