facts:
- Yarrabah is an Aboriginal Shire located on the east coast of Cape York Peninsula,
  about 50km east by south of Cairns, Queensland, Australia.
- The name Yarrabah means "place of sand and rocks" in the local Gunggandji language.
- Yarrabah is the largest discrete Indigenous community in Australia outside of the
  Northern Territory.
- The community was originally established as a mission in 1892.
- Yarrabah is known for its strong cultural traditions, including storytelling, dance,
  and music.
- The local Gunggandji people have maintained a strong connection to their land and
  sea country.
- Yarrabah has a rich artistic community, with many talented artists producing paintings,
  sculptures, and other crafts inspired by the local culture.
- The community is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, including rainforest,
  wetlands, and coastal areas.
- Fishing and crabbing are important economic activities in Yarrabah.
- Yarrabah has its own local government council, the Yarrabah Aboriginal Shire Council.
