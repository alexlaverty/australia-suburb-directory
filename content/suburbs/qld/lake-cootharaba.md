- Lake Cootharaba is the largest lake in the Noosa River system.
- The lake is popular for fishing, kayaking, and boating.
- Lake Cootharaba is part of the Great Sandy National Park.
- The area surrounding the lake is known for its diverse birdlife.
- The name "Cootharaba" is believed to be derived from an Aboriginal word meaning
  'place of flying foxes'.
- The lake is relatively shallow, with an average depth of around 1.5 meters.
- The Noosa Everglades, often called the 'River of Mirrors', flow into Lake Cootharaba.
- The lake is surrounded by various types of vegetation, including melaleuca forests,
  eucalyptus woodlands, and banksia heathlands.
- Traditional owners of the land are the Kabi Kabi people.
- Lake Cootharaba is a popular spot for watersports, including windsurfing and kitesurfing,
  when conditions are suitable.
