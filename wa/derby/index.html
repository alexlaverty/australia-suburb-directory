Facts about DERBY

  1. Derby is located in the Kimberley region of Western Australia.
  2. The town experiences a tropical savanna climate with distinct wet and dry seasons.
  3. Derby holds the record for the highest tides in Australia, reaching up to 11.8 meters.
  4. The Boab Prison Tree, a large hollow boab tree once used as a temporary lockup for Indigenous prisoners, is a significant landmark near Derby.
  5. Derby is a gateway to the Gibb River Road, a popular 4WD route.
  6. The Mowanjum Festival, celebrating Indigenous art and culture, is held annually near Derby.
  7. Derby was once a major port for the West Kimberley, but its importance declined after the development of Wyndham.
  8. The town is situated on the edge of the King Sound, known for its diverse marine life.
  9. Horizontal Falls, a natural phenomenon created by tidal currents squeezing through narrow gorges, is a popular tourist attraction accessible from Derby.
  10. Derby is home to a diverse range of bird species, making it a popular destination for birdwatchers.

Notable Historical Events in DERBY

  • Establishment as a port in 1883.
  • Construction of the Derby jetty in 1885.
  • Role in the pearling industry during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
  • Impact of World War II on the town and region.
  • Development of the Gibb River Road in the mid-20th century.
  • Establishment of the Mowanjum Community in the 1960s.
