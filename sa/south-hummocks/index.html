Facts about SOUTH HUMMOCKS

  1. South Hummocks is located in the Yorke Peninsula Council area.
  2. It is situated approximately 150km west of Adelaide, the capital city of South Australia.
  3. The area is primarily known for its agricultural activities, particularly grain farming.
  4. The Hummocks range, a series of low hills, traverses the region giving the town its name.
  5. South Hummocks is close to the coastline, offering access to beaches and fishing.
  6. The town has a small population, contributing to a rural and quiet atmosphere.
  7. Nearby towns include Kadina, Wallaroo, and Moonta, which offer additional services and amenities.
  8. The region experiences a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and cool, wet winters.
  9. The area has a history of copper mining dating back to the 19th century.
  10. South Hummocks is part of the Narungga Aboriginal nation traditional lands.
