  • It is located within Wilsons Promontory National Park.
  • Tidal River is the main hub for visitors to the park, offering accommodation, camping facilities, and a general store.
  • The town is situated at the mouth of the Tidal River, where it meets the Bass Strait.
  • Tidal River experiences a cool, temperate climate with average summer temperatures around 20°C (68°F) and average winter temperatures around 10°C (50°F).
  • The area is known for its diverse flora and fauna, including kangaroos, wallabies, wombats, emus, and various bird species.
  • Bushfires are a natural occurrence in the area and have significantly impacted the landscape in the past.
  • The Aboriginal name for Wilsons Promontory is "Wamoon", meaning "plenty of shelter".
  • Tidal River offers a range of walking trails, from short strolls to multi-day hikes.
  • The town is surrounded by pristine beaches, including Squeaky Beach, known for its fine, white sand that squeaks when walked on.
