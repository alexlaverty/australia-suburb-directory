Facts about RIVERLEIGH

  • Riverleigh is a rural locality in the North Burnett Region, Queensland, Australia.
  • In the 2016 census, Riverleigh had a population of 63 people.
  • The median age of the Riverleigh population was 52 years, 14 years above the national median of 38.
  • Children aged 0 - 14 years made up 11.1% of the population and people aged 65 years and over made up 28.6% of the population.
  • Of people in Riverleigh aged 15 years and over, 47.8% were married and 26.1% never married.
  • The main industries of employment for people aged 15 years and over, usually resident in Riverleigh, were Beef Cattle Farming (62.5%), Sheep, Beef Cattle and Grain Farming (18.8%) and Coal Mining (6.3%).
  • The median weekly personal income for people aged 15 years and over, usually resident in Riverleigh, was not stated.
  • Of employed people in Riverleigh aged 15 years and over, 12.5% worked 1 to 15 hours, 25.0% worked 16 to 29 hours and 56.3% worked 30 to 44 hours.
  • The most common industries of employment for persons aged 15 years and over, usually resident in Riverleigh were Agriculture, Forestry and Fishing at 75%, and Mining at 25%.

  • Lissner Park

Notable Historical Events in CHARTERS TOWERS CITY

  • 1871: Gold discovered at Charters Towers.
  • 1872: Charters Towers proclaimed a goldfield.
  • 1877: Charters Towers declared a municipality.
  • 1899: Charters Towers reached peak population during gold rush.
  • 1902: Great fire destroyed much of the city's business district.
  • Late 19th - early 20th century: Period of prosperity and grand architecture construction.
  • Mid-20th century: Decline in gold mining leading to economic downturn.
  • Late 20th century: Efforts to diversify the economy and promote tourism.
