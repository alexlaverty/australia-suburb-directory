Facts about DERBY

  • Derby is located in the Kimberley region of Western Australia.
  • The town experiences a tropical savanna climate with distinct wet and dry seasons.
  • Derby holds the record for the highest tides in Australia, reaching up to 11.8 meters.
  • The Boab Prison Tree, a large hollow boab tree once used as a temporary lockup for Indigenous prisoners, is a significant landmark near Derby.
  • Derby is a gateway to the Gibb River Road, a popular 4WD route.
  • The Mowanjum Festival, celebrating Indigenous art and culture, is held annually near Derby.
  • Derby was once a major port for the West Kimberley, but its importance declined after the development of Wyndham.
  • The town is situated on the edge of the King Sound, known for its diverse marine life.
  • Horizontal Falls, a natural phenomenon created by tidal currents squeezing through narrow gorges, is a popular tourist attraction accessible from Derby.
  • Derby is home to a diverse range of bird species, making it a popular destination for birdwatchers.

Tourist Locations near DERBY

  • Derby Wharf
  • Tunnel Creek
  • Windjana Gorge National Park
  • Geikie Gorge National Park
  • King Sound
  • Derby Visitor Centre

Notable Historical Events in DERBY

  • Establishment as a port in 1883.
  • Construction of the Derby jetty in 1885.
  • Role in the pearling industry during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
  • Impact of World War II on the town and region.
  • Development of the Gibb River Road in the mid-20th century.
  • Establishment of the Mowanjum Community in the 1960s.
