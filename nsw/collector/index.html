Facts about COLLECTOR

  1. Collector is located in the Upper Lachlan Shire of New South Wales, Australia.
  2. The town is situated approximately 30 km north of Goulburn and 140 km south-west of Sydney.
  3. Collector's name is believed to be derived from early settlers collecting tolls at a river crossing.
  4. The area was once inhabited by the Gundungurra Aboriginal people.
  5. Bushranger Ben Hall and his gang raided Collector in 1865.
  6. The town features historic buildings, including the Bushranger Hotel and the old police station and courthouse.
  7. Collector is known for its rural landscapes and agricultural activities.
  8. The Collector Village Pumpkin Festival is a popular annual event.
  9. The nearby Weereewa Lookout offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  10. Collector has a small, close-knit community.
