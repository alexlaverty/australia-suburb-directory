Facts about DRAKE VILLAGE

  1. Drake Village is a locality within the Tenterfield local government area of New South Wales, Australia.
  2. It is situated approximately 844km north of Sydney and 251km southwest of Brisbane.
  3. Drake Village is located near the state border between New South Wales and Queensland.
  4. The area is known for its scenic rural landscapes and proximity to national parks.
  5. The Clarence River and its tributaries flow through the region near Drake Village.
  6. The traditional custodians of the land are Aboriginal peoples, with the Bundjalung and Gumbaynggirr nations having historical connections to the area.
  7. The village likely takes its name from surveyor George Drake, who surveyed the area in the mid-19th century.
  8. The local economy has historically been based on agriculture, forestry, and mining.
  9. The region is known for its gold rush history, with the discovery of gold at Drake in the 1850s attracting prospectors to the area.
  10. The population of Drake Village and the surrounding area is relatively small.

Notable Historical Events in DRAKE VILLAGE

  • Gold was discovered in the Drake area in the 1850s, leading to a significant gold rush and population boom.
  • The construction of the Tabulam to Drake road in the late 19th century improved access to the region.
  • The establishment of the Drake Public School in 1881 marked an important milestone in the development of the local community.
