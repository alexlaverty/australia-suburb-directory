Facts about MURRUMBURRAH

  1. Murrumburrah is located in the Hilltops Region of New South Wales, Australia.
  2. The town is situated on the Burley Griffin Way, approximately halfway between Sydney and Melbourne.
  3. Murrumburrah is part of the twin towns of Harden-Murrumburrah, with Harden being the larger of the two.
  4. Historically, the area was inhabited by the Wiradjuri Aboriginal people.
  5. Murrumburrah was officially proclaimed a town in 1863.
  6. The town's name is believed to be derived from an Aboriginal word meaning 'two heads' or 'meeting of the waters'.
  7. Murrumburrah railway station opened in 1877 and served as an important transport hub.
  8. The local economy is primarily based on agriculture, with sheep and wheat farming being prominent.
  9. The town features several historic buildings, including the Murrumburrah Courthouse and the former Commercial Bank.
  10. Murrumburrah Public School was established in 1878.
