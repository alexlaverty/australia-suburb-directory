Facts about DAWES POINT

  1. Dawes Point is located at the northern tip of the Sydney central business district.
  2. It is named after Lieutenant William Dawes, who established an observatory there in 1788.
  3. Dawes Point is home to the southern pylon of the Sydney Harbour Bridge.
  4. The area is part of the traditional lands of the Gadigal people.
  5. Dawes Point Park offers stunning views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge.
  6. The area has a mix of historical buildings and modern apartments.
  7. It is a popular spot for tourists and photographers.
  8. The suburb is within walking distance of many of Sydney's main attractions.
  9. Barangaroo Reserve, a significant regenerated parkland area, is located adjacent to Dawes Point.
  10. Dawes Point Battery, a former defensive position, is a historical site within the suburb.

Tourist Locations near DAWES POINT

  1. Sydney Harbour Bridge Pylon Lookout
  2. Dawes Point Battery

Notable Historical Events in DAWES POINT

  • Establishment of an observatory by Lieutenant William Dawes in 1788
  • Construction of the southern pylon of the Sydney Harbour Bridge in the late 1920s
  • Opening of the Sydney Harbour Bridge in 1932
  • Creation of Barangaroo Reserve in the 2010s
