Facts about THE PILLIGA

  1. The Pilliga is actually a very large area encompassing several small villages and localities.
  2. The Pilliga forest is one of the largest continuous areas of native bushland in the western plains of NSW.
  3. The area is known for its diverse flora and fauna, including koalas, kangaroos, emus, and numerous bird species.
  4. The Pilliga is part of the traditional lands of the Gamilaraay people.
  5. The Pilliga Scrub is a significant part of the Murray-Darling Basin and plays a crucial role in water management.
  6. The Pilliga has a history of forestry and timber production.
  7. The area is known for its hot summers and mild winters.
  8. The Pilliga is home to the Pilliga sandstone caves, some of which contain Aboriginal rock art.
  9. The name "Pilliga" is believed to be derived from an Aboriginal word meaning "scrub" or "thick forest."
  10. The Pilliga has a small, scattered population, with many residents living on rural properties.

Notable Historical Events in THE PILLIGA

  • The establishment of the Pilliga State Forest in the early 20th century.
  • The construction of the Baradine Dam in the mid-20th century.
  • Ongoing debates and protests surrounding coal seam gas exploration in the Pilliga.
