Facts about EAST KURRAJONG

  1. East Kurrajong is a semi-rural suburb located in the Hawkesbury region of New South Wales, Australia.
  2. It is situated approximately 70 kilometers northwest of Sydney.
  3. The suburb is known for its scenic landscapes, featuring rolling hills, bushland, and views of the Blue Mountains.
  4. East Kurrajong is home to a number of small farms and orchards.
  5. The area has a rich history, with evidence of Aboriginal occupation dating back thousands of years.
  6. East Kurrajong was originally part of a larger land grant made to Governor Philip Gidley King in 1804.
  7. The suburb's name is derived from the Aboriginal word 'garrajang', which is believed to mean 'edible plants'.
  8. East Kurrajong Public School was established in 1887.
  9. The area is popular with bushwalkers and nature enthusiasts.
  10. East Kurrajong is part of the Hawkesbury City local government area.
