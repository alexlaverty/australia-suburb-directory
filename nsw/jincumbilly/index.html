Facts about JINCUMBILLY

  1. Jincumbilly is a small rural locality in the Snowy Monaro Regional Council area of New South Wales, Australia.
  2. It is located approximately 35 kilometers south of Cooma and 15 kilometers east of Nimmitabel.
  3. The name Jincumbilly is believed to be derived from an Aboriginal word meaning 'plenty of possums'.
  4. The area was traditionally occupied by the Ngarigo Aboriginal people.
  5. The Jincumbilly Caves are a popular tourist attraction in the area.
  6. The caves are home to a variety of cave formations, including stalactites, stalagmites, and flowstones.
  7. The surrounding area is known for its natural beauty, with rolling hills, forests, and clear streams.
  8. The area is popular for outdoor activities such as bushwalking, camping, and fishing.
  9. The Jincumbilly Post Office opened on 1 October 1913 and closed in 1970.
  10. Jincumbilly is close to the Snowy Mountains Scheme and was impacted by the construction activities.
