Facts about TOOBEAH

  1. Toobeah is located in the Goondiwindi Region of Queensland, Australia.
  2. The town is situated on the western bank of the Macintyre River, which forms the border between Queensland and New South Wales.
  3. Toobeah's name is believed to be derived from an Aboriginal word meaning "place of edible roots."
  4. The area was originally occupied by the Bigambul Aboriginal people.
  5. Toobeah was established as a settlement in the late 19th century, primarily to serve the growing pastoral industry.
  6. The town's economy is still largely based on agriculture, with cotton, wheat, and beef cattle being the main products.
  7. Toobeah has a small population, estimated to be less than 100 people as of 2021.
  8. The town has a general store, a hotel, and a primary school.
  9. Toobeah experiences a hot and dry climate, typical of inland Queensland.
  10. Toobeah is part of the electorate of Maranoa in the Australian House of Representatives.
